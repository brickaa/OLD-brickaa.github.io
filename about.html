Becca Aaronson

News apps producer and co-leader of The Texas Tribune's news apps team.

As a founding member of the The Texas Tribune’s news apps team, I've helped progress the Tribune's mission to inform and engage Texas about public policy, politics, government and statewide issues. We develop news applications — visual features and data-driven editorial content for special investigative projects.

I joined the Tribune in 2010, shortly after earning a bachelor’s degree in cultural theory from Scripps College in Claremont, CA. As a data reporter, I learned to develop interactive stories, and wrote about a myriad of political topics, including the economy, health care and the 2011 Texas legislative session. From 2012 to early 2014, my beat was health care, and during that time I was nationally recognized for my coverage of women’s health and abortion politics.

In my unprofessional life, I'm a super nerdy lady from Austin. I have two cats, Stella and Lola. They're the stars of my Instagram account, and my life. On the weekends, you'll find me on my bike exploring. I like growing delicious vegetables in my garden. Also, I'm pretty sure I could live on tacos.
